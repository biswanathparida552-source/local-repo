1. What is BRICS?

Definition: BRICS is an international group of five major emerging economies - Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa.

Origin: The term ‚ÄúBRIC‚Äù was first coined in 2001 by economist Jim O'Neill of Goldman Sachs to highlight the growing importance of these economies. South Africa joined in 2010, making it BRICS.

Objective: To promote economic cooperation, political dialogue, and a multipolar world order (reducing dominance of Western powers like the USA and EU).

Institutions:

  • New Development Bank (NDB) - provides funding for infrastructure and sustainable projects.
  • Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA) - financial safety net during crises.
  • Annual BRICS Summit - leaders meet every year to discuss global issues.

3. Future of BRICS

Economic Growth: BRICS economies together represent 40% of the world's population and over 30% of global GDP, and this share is expected to grow.

Expansion: Recently, countries like Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iran, Egypt, and Ethiopia were invited to join (BRICS+), making it a larger economic bloc.

Alternative Finance: Push to reduce dependence on the US dollar by using local currencies in trade.

Technology & Innovation: Collaboration in AI, digital economy, renewable energy, and space.

Challenges:

  • Political differences among members (e.g., China-India border tensions).
  • Dependence on global trade systems still dominated by the West.

Overall Future: BRICS aims to become a powerful alternative to G7, shaping a multipolar world order, focusing on development, energy security, and fair global governance.
